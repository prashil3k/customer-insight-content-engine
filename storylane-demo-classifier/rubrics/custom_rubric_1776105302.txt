# Product Demo Evaluation Rubric: Social Proof Showcase

This rubric evaluates interactive product demos based on how effectively they demonstrate social proof and use "show don't tell" principles to build credibility and trust with potential users.

## Classification Buckets

### **Social Proof Champion**
Demos that masterfully integrate multiple forms of social proof throughout the experience
- Real customer testimonials, reviews, or case studies prominently featured
- Live usage metrics, user counts, or activity feeds visible
- Customer logos, certifications, or awards displayed contextually
- User-generated content or community elements showcased

### **Trust Builder**
Demos that include solid social proof elements but in a more traditional way
- Customer testimonials present but static
- Some metrics or numbers shown (users, companies served, etc.)
- Customer logos displayed but not integrated into the demo flow
- Basic credibility indicators like security badges or certifications

### **Feature Focused**
Demos that prioritize product functionality with minimal social proof
- Primarily demonstrates product capabilities and features
- Limited or superficial social proof elements
- Focus on "what it does" rather than "who uses it"
- May mention customers but doesn't showcase their success

### **Generic Showcase**
Demos that show the product but lack compelling social validation
- Basic product walkthrough without customer context
- No visible user metrics or social indicators
- Generic messaging without specific customer examples
- Misses opportunities to demonstrate real-world usage

### **Gated / Inaccessible**
Demos that cannot be properly evaluated due to access restrictions
- Requires login, registration, or special permissions
- Broken links or technical issues preventing access
- Geographic restrictions or other barriers to viewing

## Evaluation Dimensions

**social_proof_strength**: How compelling and prominent the social proof elements are (1 = no social proof visible, 10 = multiple strong forms of social proof seamlessly integrated)

**credibility_building**: How effectively the demo builds trust and credibility (1 = undermines confidence, 10 = builds strong confidence in the product and company)

**integration_quality**: How well social proof elements are woven into the demo experience (1 = disconnected or forced, 10 = naturally integrated and contextual)

**evidence_authenticity**: How genuine and specific the social proof appears (1 = generic or fake-seeming, 10 = clearly authentic with specific details)

**visual_impact**: How effectively visuals and interactivity showcase social proof (1 = all text-based or poorly presented, 10 = compelling visuals that show real usage)

**persuasive_power**: Overall ability to convince prospects through demonstrated success (1 = fails to persuade, 10 = highly convincing through proven results)

## Output Format

Respond in JSON:
{
  "type": "one of the classification buckets above",
  "overall_score": 1-10,
  "social_proof_strength": 1-10,
  "credibility_building": 1-10,
  "integration_quality": 1-10,
  "evidence_authenticity": 1-10,
  "visual_impact": 1-10,
  "persuasive_power": 1-10,
  "summary": "2-3 sentence summary of what the demo shows and how it tells its story",
  "strengths": ["list of specific things done well"],
  "weaknesses": ["list of specific areas for improvement"],
  "recommendation": "One specific actionable suggestion to improve this demo"
}
